BUSINESS-SPECIFIC OPS — SALON / SPA / BARBER (vertical selectable)
================================================================
WHAT "AFTER THE SITE" MEANS
The site shows the service menu (with "from" pricing) + lookbook and captures
booking requests. The economics are repeat visits and a full chair — no-shows
and gaps are the enemy. The ops job is to confirm the booking, prevent the
no-show, earn the review, and bring the client back on cadence.

LEAD INTENTS (intents[])
- "booking" (P1) — wants an appointment.
- "consult" (P2) — colour / treatment consult.
- "gift-card" (P3) — purchase / question.
- "general" (P3).

CRM PIPELINE (stages[])
inquiry -> booked -> seen -> rebook-due -> won / lost.

FOLLOW-UP (followUp)
Fire immediately. Confirm the slot + stylist + prep notes, and a reminder a day
before to cut no-shows. Where the business takes deposits on long services, link
the deposit. delayHours: 0.

REVIEW REQUEST (reviewRequest)
trigger: "job-complete" mapped to SEEN. Ask same-day or next-day while they love
the result. Platform: GOOGLE_REVIEW_URL. No incentives.

INVOICING / REMINDERS (invoice)
Most services are pay-at-visit, so repurpose the invoice workflow as a REBOOKING
reminder (e.g. 4-6 weeks after a cut/colour) and a NO-SHOW / late-cancel deposit
notice. reminderDays map to the rebooking cadence.

COMPLIANCE FOR OUTBOUND
Public-health personal-service-setting (PSS) standards are an in-shop matter;
keep marketing honest (no medical claims for aesthetics). CASL consent +
unsubscribe; gift-card terms where relevant.

VERTICAL AUTOMATIONS (optional n8n additions)
- Repurpose workflow (c) as a rebooking cadence keyed off last-visit date.
- A 24h appointment reminder keyed off the booked slot (reuse the schedule
  trigger) to protect against no-shows.
