BUSINESS-SPECIFIC OPS — EVENT VENUE / PHOTOGRAPHER
================================================================
WHAT "AFTER THE SITE" MEANS
The site captures date inquiries (date / guest count / budget) and shows
portfolio galleries + packages. Bookings are high-value, far in advance, and
date-exclusive — two couples can't have the same Saturday. The ops job is to
respond before the date gets booked elsewhere, convert the tour/consult into a
signed contract + deposit, then collect installments and earn the post-event
review that drives the next year's bookings.

LEAD INTENTS (intents[])
- "date-inquiry" (P1) — checking a specific date; respond same day.
- "tour" (P2) — wants to see the venue / meet the photographer.
- "package" (P2) — pricing / package question.
- "general" (P3).

CRM PIPELINE (stages[])
inquiry -> consult/tour -> proposal -> booked -> event-complete -> won / lost.
(event-complete is the job-complete equivalent for the review step.)

FOLLOW-UP (followUp)
Fire immediately and lead with availability for the requested date (scarcity is
real and honest here). Attach/link the relevant package + portfolio. Offer the
tour/consult booking link. delayHours: 0.

REVIEW REQUEST (reviewRequest)
trigger: "job-complete" mapped to EVENT-COMPLETE. Ask a few days after, with the
gallery/delivery — emotion is highest then. Platform: GOOGLE_REVIEW_URL (and
optionally a wedding directory as a second link). No incentives.

INVOICING / REMINDERS (invoice)
Milestone invoicing: deposit to hold the date -> installment(s) -> final balance
before the event. reminderDays apply to each milestone's due date; never let the
final balance lapse past the event.

COMPLIANCE FOR OUTBOUND
Clear deposit / cancellation terms referenced (contract governs). Any liquor
licence / capacity certificate noted where applicable (TODO until provided).
CASL consent + unsubscribe.

VERTICAL AUTOMATIONS (optional n8n additions)
- Add installment milestones as scheduled checkpoints in workflow (c).
- A pre-event countdown touch (final details / timeline) keyed off the event
  date, reusing the schedule trigger.
